11. What WhatCable is, and is not

This section matters. Please read it.

WhatCable is a diagnostic aid. It reports information that macOS makes available about your ports, cables, chargers and connected devices, and explains it in plain English. It is not a test instrument, and it does not physically measure a cable.

Much of what WhatCable shows comes from data the cable or device reports about itself, such as a USB-C e-marker chip. A cable can misreport its own capabilities, whether through a manufacturing fault, a design shortcut or deliberate misrepresentation. WhatCable can only tell you what was reported and whether it matches what the connection actually achieved.

It follows that:

  • WhatCable output is not a certification. It is not a USB Implementers Forum certification, a conformance or compliance test, or proof that a cable is genuine, counterfeit, safe or unsafe. Do not present it as any of those, to a supplier, a customer or anyone else.
  • Findings describe data, not manufacturers. Where WhatCable flags a reading as unusual or inconsistent, that is a statement about the data read from that specific unit on that specific connection. It is not an assertion about any named manufacturer, brand or product line, and must not be represented as one.
  • Do not rely on it alone for consequential decisions. WhatCable must not be the sole basis for any decision where being wrong could cause injury, property damage or financial loss. Verify independently.
  • Not for safety-critical use. WhatCable is not designed, tested or certified for use in medical, aviation, automotive, maritime, industrial control, nuclear or any other environment where failure could lead to death, injury or serious harm. Do not use it in those settings.
